Feature 01

Onboarding & Login

Email-based, seeded from the attendee list. Our app and the client's onboarding system are fully isolated — no integration between them.

Business Goal

Get a registered attendee into the app quickly with a simple email login. The client provides a one-time attendee list (Name, Email, Role, Day Access) that we maintain in our own database. Login runs entirely on our side; the list acts as an allowlist.

User Need

The attendee wants to get into the app fast by entering the email they registered with — no sign-up forms, no passwords, no identity proofing.

Design Requirements
  • No client integration. We do not receive a token, session, or hand-off from the client's systems.
  • We maintain our own record: Name, Email (unique key), Role, Day Access — seeded from the client's attendee list.
  • Email login, allowlist-gated: enter email → match against seeded list → if found, Name, Role, and Day Access are read from the record (never typed).
  • OTP ownership verification: one-time code emailed to the attendee. Design both entry and resend flows.
  • Role drives access. Elevated roles (speaker / staff / organiser / MC) unlock admin routes directly — no separate admin login.
  • Day Access drives agenda visibility. The Day Access field on the record gates what agenda days the attendee can see (see Feature 2).
  • QR fallback pass: app-generated QR, on-device, renders fully offline. One tap from Home / profile.

Feature 02 Updated

Agenda

The highest daily-usage surface. Day Access is multi-value and role-aware — attendees see only the day(s) their ticket covers; elevated roles bypass gating. Excel bulk upload confirmed.

v4 Clarification Day Access is multi-value. An attendee's record can carry multiple days (e.g. Day 1 + Day 2). The day-selector renders only the tabs that the attendee's record permits — unregistered days are absent from the UI entirely, not greyed out. Elevated roles (Speaker / Staff / Organiser / MC) bypass day-access gating and see all available days. Cross-day access is blocked server-side — a direct API call for an unregistered day is rejected at the data layer, not only in the UI.
Business Goal

Keep attendees informed of the schedule for the day(s) they are registered for. The agenda is the highest daily-usage surface — if it fails or feels slow, the app feels broken.

User Need

Attendees need to quickly find what is happening now, what is coming up next, and plan their day. They may be standing in a hallway with 30 seconds to make a decision.

Design Requirements
  • "Now" and "Next" sessions must be visible without any scrolling.
  • Day Access is multi-value: an attendee's record may grant access to one or more days (e.g. Day 1 only, Day 2 only, or Day 1 + Day 2). The day-selector tabs render only the permitted days — not greyed out or hidden behind a lock, simply not rendered.
  • Elevated roles bypass day gating entirely: Speaker, Staff, Organiser, and MC roles see all available days without restriction.
  • Server-side enforcement is mandatory. Unregistered day content must be blocked at the API layer — the UI restriction alone is not sufficient.
  • Excel bulk upload (confirmed): each day's sessions are uploaded by the organiser via a fixed-format Excel in the admin tool. Our importer is the system of record.
  • Upcoming days not yet uploaded must read as "coming soon," never as an error or blank.
  • The schedule is live — changes can happen mid-event. Communicate updates without alarming users.
  • Time always displays in venue-local time zone. Never convert to the device's time zone.
  • Agenda must be fully usable offline using the last cached version.
  • Room name on a session is tappable → deep-links to the Wayfinder.

Feature 06 Updated

Event Photos

AI face matching via a third-party cloud tool (TurtlePic TBC) is the confirmed baseline. Matched photos are distributed strictly to the matched attendee's account — no cross-account access. My Photos is powered by a selfie the attendee uploads in-app, purely for photo matching.

v4 Clarification — AI pipeline & isolation model confirmed AI face matching runs via a third-party cloud tool (TurtlePic TBC), not an on-device or in-house system. The flow: Admin bulk-uploads all event photos → the cloud AI scans them against each attendee's on-file selfie → matched photos are distributed only to that attendee's account. Strict per-account isolation: no attendee can view or access another attendee's matched photos. Manual tagging remains as a fallback. This is entirely independent of the client's facial-recognition system. Vendor (TurtlePic) is pending confirmation — see Open Question 10.
Business Goal

Give attendees an effortless, personal way to find and keep photos of themselves from the event, driving engagement and social sharing.

Design Requirements
  • AI face matching — baseline via third-party cloud tool (TurtlePic TBC). Attendee uploads a selfie in-app → uploaded to the cloud AI service → service scans all admin-uploaded event photos → matched photos are pushed only to that attendee's account in My Photos.
  • Strict per-account isolation. Matched photos are distributed exclusively to the attendee whose selfie produced the match. No attendee can view, browse, or access another attendee's matched photos at any point.
  • Requires its own consent + privacy disclaimer screen (how the selfie is used, which third-party processes it, when it will be deleted) with a clear Skip option that falls back to manual tagging.
  • Manual self-tagging remains available: attendee taps photos they are in to add to My Photos.
  • Each matched photo has a one-tap "This isn't me" report.
  • Opt-out deletes the uploaded selfie and all resulting matches permanently.
  • Photos are uploaded by the on-site editing team day-wise via admin bulk upload — gallery communicates daily batches.
  • Gallery is a thumbnail grid, paginated; full-resolution on tap. Each photo supports: My Photos, download, share, hide.
  • Completely independent of the client's facial-recognition system.

Feature 09 Updated

Live Polls

MC-controlled filler content between sessions. Generic / event-wide — not session-specific. No push notification.

v3 Changes Polls are generic and event-wide — not attached to sessions. They are MC-controlled only (not speakers or attendees). No push notification for new polls. Each poll has a 2–5 minute response window. Polls are filler content the MC uses between sessions to maintain engagement.
v4 Confirmed Live Polls are confirmed as MC-controlled filler / engagement content. Their purpose is to keep the audience active during breaks and gaps between sessions — not to collect feedback, survey data, or session-specific sentiment. Design must reflect this intent: polls should feel low-friction, lightweight, and dismissible. They are not a primary feature and should never compete visually with the Agenda or live session content.
Business Goal

Drive real-time audience participation during breaks and gaps between sessions. Give the MC a tool to keep the room engaged and surface instant sentiment data.

User Need — Attendee

When a poll is active on screen during a break, the attendee wants to vote quickly and see how the rest of the room voted — without friction.

User Need — MC

Prepare questions in advance, launch the poll from the admin tool at the right moment, watch votes come in live, and close the poll when the 2–5 min window ends.

Design Requirements
  • Event-wide only — polls are not targeted at specific sessions or session audiences.
  • MC-controlled. Only the MC role can create, launch, and close polls from the admin tool. Speakers and attendees have no poll controls.
  • Questions are prepared in advance in the admin tool, not spontaneous.
  • No push notification for new polls. Polls appear as an in-app bottom sheet when the attendee is idle, or a dismissible banner when mid-task.
  • 2–5 minute response window. A visible countdown timer on both the attendee poll and the MC's live results dashboard.
  • Live results must animate — bar percentages update visually as votes come in.
  • Show "X people voted" below results for social proof.
  • Attendees cannot vote more than once.
  • Admin poll controls (Launch, Close) require explicit confirm dialogs.
